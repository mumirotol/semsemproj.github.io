1.Opportunity

Opportunity, also referred to as MER-B or MER-1, is a robotic rover that explored Mars from 2004 to 2018, operating for 5111 sols on the planet. NASA's Mars rover, Opportunity, was suddenly engulfed by a dust storm, that is when it sent a message home saying: "My battery is low, and it's getting dark."

This was the last we heard from Opportunity, and despite efforts made for contact, NASA was confident that Opportunity's solar panel are damaged. 2.Challenger Disaster

On January 28, 1986, the Space Shuttle Challenger tragically disintegrated 73 seconds after liftoff, claiming the lives of all seven crew members. The shuttle broke apart at an altitude of 46,000 feet above the Atlantic Ocean, near Cape Canaveral, Florida, at 11:39 a.m. EST. A congressional investigation into the incident, witnessed by millions on live television, determined that the disaster was "not survivable."

The recovered remains of the crew are interred at Arlington National Cemetery. Following the tragedy, President Ronald Reagan formed a commission to investigate.
